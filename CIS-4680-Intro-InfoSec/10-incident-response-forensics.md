# Lab 10 – Incident Response Procedures, Forensics, and Forensic Analysis

**Course:** CIS 4680 – Introduction to Information Security and Privacy

**Platform:** ACI Learning Skill Labs

## Objective
The objective of this lab was to understand the fundamentals of incident response procedures and digital forensics. The lab focused on identifying security incidents, collecting forensic evidence, preserving data integrity, and analyzing compromised systems.

## Tools & Technologies Used
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Windows Event Viewer | Log-based investigation |
| `grep`, `awk`, `sort` | Log parsing and filtering |

## Key Concepts Covered
- **Incident Response Lifecycle**
- **Evidence Collection and Preservation**
- **Digital Forensics Fundamentals**
- **Log Analysis**
- **File System Analysis**

## What I Learned
- Through this lab, I learned how organizations respond to cybersecurity incidents using structured procedures and forensic techniques. I gained hands-on experience analyzing logs, capturing evidence, and identifying suspicious activity within a system. I also learned the importance of maintaining evidence integrity and documenting investigative findings during an incident response process.

## Real-World Application
The skills practiced in this lab are directly applicable to cybersecurity roles such as Security Analyst, Incident Responder, and Digital Forensics Analyst. These procedures are commonly used in enterprise environments to investigate security breaches, detect malicious activity, and support recovery efforts after cyberattacks.
